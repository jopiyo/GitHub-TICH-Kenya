# Whitepaper: Data-Driven Climate Adaptation in Community Health

**Author:** TICH-Kenya Research Team  
**Date:** April 2026

## Executive Summary
Traditional health systems in East Africa are reactive. ClimateGuard proposes a shift to **Anticipatory Action**. By leveraging the "Digital Epidemiology" framework pioneered by TICH, we can treat climate data as a leading indicator for public health crises.

## The Problem
In the Lake Victoria Basin, malaria transmission peaks are shifting due to altered rainfall cycles. Current interventions often arrive *after* the peak.

## The Solution: The "Loop" Model
1.  **Sense:** Continuous monitoring of environmental variables.
2.  **Analyze:** Comparing real-time data against 10-year epidemiological baselines.
3.  **Act:** Triggering community-level interventions (distribution of nets, water treatment) *before* the climate-induced spike occurs.
